Compound Interest 1: First Practice

You: Hey Mr Scott, My stationary business is a hit! I wanted to expand, would you lend me $1000 again?

Mr. Scott: Sure, But now, the cost of borrowing has changed!

You: Have you increased the rate of interest Mr. Scott?

Mr. Scott: No, the rate of interest is still 10%. But now, I also charge interest on interest.

You: Interest on interest? What does that mean?

Mr. Scott: That means, you would have to pay 10% on the $1000 that I lend you, AND 10% on the $100 interest.

You: So for the second year, I pay interest on $1100 instead of $1000?

Mr. Scott: That's correct, that is called compound interest!
